Intelligent System for Alzheimer's Disease Diagnosis and Management

Literature Survey




Welcome to our research website

Alzheimer's disease (AD) represents the leading cause of dementia in elderly adults worldwide, impacting more than 55 million patients globally and occurring in 60%-70% of dementia cases. AD manifests as a progressive neurodegenerative disorder that produces beta-amyloid plaques together with neurofibrillary tangles during its initial stages, focusing damage on the hippocampus and entorhinal cortex before affecting wider areas of the cortex.

The pathological development initiates a chain of detrimental processes affecting brain cells that lead to synaptic loss and cellular breakdown, resulting in clinical symptoms of memory impairment, cognitive deterioration, behavioral changes, and diminished functional independence. The disease-related socioeconomic burden causes extensive emotional distress and heavy financial costs, affecting healthcare organizations, economies, patients, and caregivers worldwide.

The precise and prompt diagnosis of AD presents significant challenges. Current medical examinations, cognitive tests, and structural imaging procedures lack the ability to detect AD until after extensive neurodegeneration occurs, which typically starts several years before clinical manifestations appear. Confidence levels of clinical diagnosis stand between 60-70% according to post-mortem assessments, highlighting the limitations of present-day diagnostic tools.

To address these challenges, this research proposes an intelligent system that combines several significant aspects: MRI analysis for early detection and staging of the disease, emotion recognition and response analysis, risk assessment, and memory therapy sessions. The system employs advanced deep learning algorithms to analyze brain MRIs, recognize emotional states, assess risk factors, and provide cognitive enhancement exercises—delivering a comprehensive approach to Alzheimer's care from diagnosis through treatment.

System Components




1
MRI Analysis for Early Detection and Staging of Alzheimer's Disease

This component applies advanced deep learning techniques to assess structural MRI scans, achieving 92.5% accuracy in diagnosing and staging Alzheimer's disease. The EfficientNetV2B0 architecture analyzes structural brain changes in critical regions including the hippocampus, entorhinal cortex, temporal lobe, and ventricular system. The system generates quantitative biomarkers focusing on hippocampal volume loss and ventricular expansion, which strongly correlate with cognitive decline. Images are classified into four categories: non-demented (normal control), very mild dementia, mild dementia, and moderate dementia.

2
Emotion Recognition & Response System

This advanced, integrated platform enriches patients' emotional and social well-being through state-of-the-art technologies. The system combines facial emotion recognition using deep CNNs (ResNet50 and VGG16) and speech emotion recognition utilizing machine learning techniques (Random Forest and SVM). Both components analyze seven emotion classes: angry, disgust, fear, happy, neutral, sad, and surprise. The system's unique feature is its response system that dynamically adjusts to produce tailored interventions based on the patient's detected emotional state, particularly supporting patients who can no longer effectively communicate their feelings.

3
Alzheimer's Risk Prediction System

This component develops a predictive model for assessing an individual's risk of developing Alzheimer's disease based on 32 health and lifestyle parameters. Using a dataset of 2,149 records containing demographic details, lifestyle factors, medical history, and cognitive indicators, the system trained multiple machine learning models. The Random Forest algorithm was selected as the best performer with 96.3% validation accuracy. The system provides actionable recommendations based on risk levels: high-risk patients (>75%) are directed to MRI analysis, while moderate-risk patients (50-75%) receive cognitive therapy suggestions. Each assessment is stored for longitudinal tracking and professional review.

4
Memory Therapy and Cognitive Enhancement

This comprehensive cognitive rehabilitation component integrates various therapeutic approaches including game-based memory exercises, picture recognition tasks, step identification activities, and voice-based storytelling. The system personalizes therapy through advanced algorithms including Finite State Machines, Needleman-Wunsch algorithms, and NLP-based storytelling techniques. Patient engagement and cognitive improvement are tracked through multiple metrics: accuracy, response times, and task completion rates. The therapy shows significant impact with 78.5% user engagement and 82.3% task completion rates. This component builds on research showing that both paper-pencil and computer-based cognitive training improve memory and cognitive function in Alzheimer's patients.

Research Problem & Solution




Proposed Problem

TThe diagnostic problems together with the intricate character of AD progression call for fresh approaches in both detection and management of the disease. Current medical examinations together with cognitive tests and structural imaging procedures lack the ability to detect AD until after extensive neurodegeneration occurs, which typically starts several years before clinical manifestations appear. The confidence levels of clinical diagnosis stand between 60-70% according to post-mortem assessments, which demonstrates a problem with present-day diagnostic tools. Additionally, other neurodegenerative disorders share similar characteristics with AD, making early therapeutic intervention limited.

Proposed Solution

TOur research proposes an inclusive AI system that combines four essential components which form a systematic strategy for managing AD from early detection to ongoing treatment and monitoring. The MRI analysis component employs deep learning with EfficientNetV2B0 architecture to evaluate structural brain changes across key regions. Our emotion recognition system analyzes facial expressions and vocal patterns to determine patient emotional states and provide supportive responses. The risk assessment module processes demographic data, medical history, cognitive test scores, and lifestyle factors to predict disease risk levels. Finally, our memory therapy component offers specialized cognitive exercises for patients at different stages. The integration of these components creates a comprehensive solution that addresses the full spectrum of Alzheimer's care with high accuracy, personalization, and technological innovation.

Methodology




MRI Analysis: Our methodology includes comprehensive preprocessing of T1-weighted MRI scans, including skull stripping, bias field correction, and spatial normalization. The EfficientNetV2B0 architecture analyzes MRI features focusing on the hippocampus, entorhinal cortex, temporal lobe, and ventricular system. The system generates quantitative biomarkers with 92.5% accuracy across four categories of diagnosis.

Emotion Recognition: We utilize two datasets: a facial emotion dataset and a speech emotion dataset, both classifying seven emotional states. For facial analysis, we use CNN architectures (ResNet50 and VGG16), while speech analysis employs both traditional machine learning (SVM, Random Forest) and neural networks with MFCC feature extraction.

Risk Prediction: Our approach analyzes 32 attributes across 2,149 records, including demographic details, lifestyle factors, medical history, and cognitive indicators. After comparing multiple algorithms, we selected the Random Forest model for its superior 96.3% accuracy.

Memory Therapy: We employ a hybrid research approach combining qualitative interviews with quantitative performance tracking. Therapy is personalized using Finite State Machines, Needleman-Wunsch algorithms, and NLP-based storytelling, with comprehensive metrics tracking engagement and improvement.
